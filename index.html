About Sudha Murthy

Author, philanthropist, educator, engineer, and public speaker, Sudha Murthy has won numerous accolades. She has written over 40 books, including the bestselling Three Thousand Stitches, Dollar Bahu, and Wise and Otherwise. She is also a renowned author of children's books like Grandma‚Äôs Bag of Stories, Gopi Diaries, How I Taught My Grandmother to Read, and many more.

Sudha Murthy began her writing career in 1978 with a Kannada travelogue Atlanticadacheyinda. Her first English book, Wise and Otherwise, was published in 2002. Her books have sold over 6 million copies worldwide and have been translated into several Indian languages, making her work accessible to diverse audiences.

Her storytelling, inspired by her life experiences and mythology, aims to instill values like gratitude, hard work, compassion, and empathy in children and adults alike. Several of her books have been adapted into popular TV series, films, and animations, including Dollar Bahu on Zee TV and the Netflix series The Bird with the Golden Wings.

Her exceptional contribution to literature and social work has earned her prestigious awards, including the Sahitya Akademi Award (2023), R.K. Narayan Award (2006), and the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Award (2020). Sudha Murthy also holds 10 honorary doctorates in literature from various universities in India.
